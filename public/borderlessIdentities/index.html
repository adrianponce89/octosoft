Borderless Identities

Borderless Identities is an up and coming Latinx LGBTQ+ Non-Profit Organization based around the San Francisco Bay Area, that dedicates itself to bridging the cultural divide between LGBTQ+ Latinxs and Cishet Latinxs, as well as running digital fundraisers and support in any and all technological endeavors for fundraisers that impassion them the most. Some of the causes assisted by Borderless Identities have been the Fundraiser for Sean Monterrosa's family, various fundraisers relating to the Dolores Day Labor Program, the macro Black Trans Lives movement, multiple fundraisers for El Comedor Monseor Romero, and the #SaveKorova Campaign that helped save Multiespacio Korova, an important Argentinian Cultural Center.

Octosoft, Korova and Community Services

Borderless Identities currently runs two mobile soup kitchens in Buenos Aires, Argentina, aimed at helping the increasing homeless population access free food to get through the week. Borderless Identities has also entered a long term partnership with Multiespacio Korova to produce Digital Events on Facebook, Youtube, Twitch and some privater mediums. After closing that partnership with Multiespacio Korova, Xoakin (along with their partners) unified Octosoft Professionals previously established probono program to sponsor Borderless Identities digital efforts. As an exclusively volunteer based organization, Borderless Identities has grown to a substantial number of team members, at a massive number of 37 volunteers all over the American continent. If you want volunteer, donate or request for community based assistance, feel free to contact Borderless Identities at borderlessidentities@gmail.com or by calling +1 (415) 286-4255.

Pride and Growth

Borderless Identities was born through Xoakin R's and Mercedes Perez' desire to help bring their local LGBTQ+ Latinx Community together through COVID-19 with Art, Music and an amazing time, and thus, the first annual Pride Out Rage Festival was born! After the event, local recognition grew and interest in what we did increased. Mercedes transitioned to other project while Xoakin moved forward and began the long and arduous road of filing for a 501c3 and well on its way to be a fully fledged Non-Profit Organization.
